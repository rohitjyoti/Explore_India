India Gate
Facts
  • The Duke of Connaught laid down the foundation stone of the India Gate - War Memorial on February 10 1921
  • It is one of the largest war graves of soldiers killed in the First World War
  • Pink and yellow Bharatpur stones were used to construct the entire arch
About

India Gate is a war memorial in New Delhi, built to commemorate the Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Afghan Wars. Designed by Edwin Lutyens and completed in 1931, it is a 42-meter-high archway made of red and yellow sandstone. The names of more than 13,500 servicemen who died are inscribed on the walls, and two pavilions house the eternal flame. The monument is surrounded by gardens and fountains, which are especially beautiful when lit up in the evenings. India Gate is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of India's rich history and culture. Nearby landmarks include the Rashtrapati Bhavan and the Parliament House, and the nearby Rajpath road is a popular place for parades and national celebrations.

When should Visit

India Gate, located in the heart of New Delhi, is open 24 hours a day. The best time to visit is during the cooler months of October to March, in the early morning or evening to avoid crowds and experience the monument's beauty illuminated at night.

Transportation

By Metro:
Delhi Metro is a convenient and affordable way to reach India Gate. The closest metro stations are Central Secretariat, Khan Market, and Udyog Bhawan, which are all located on the Yellow Line. From there, you can take a short taxi or auto-rickshaw ride to reach the monument.

By Bus:
Delhi Transport Corporation (DTC) buses and local buses connect India Gate with various parts of the city. The closest bus stops are India Gate and Tilak Marg.

By Car:
If you have your vehicle, you can drive to India Gate. There is a parking area available, but it can be crowded during peak hours.

By Tourist-Bus:
Many tour operators offer guided tours of Delhi that include a visit to India Gate. This is a convenient option for visitors who want to explore the city's sights without worrying about transportation.

By Auto-rickshaws and Taxi:
Taxis and auto-rickshaws are readily available in Delhi and can take you directly to India Gate. It's advisable to negotiate the fare before starting the journey to avoid any confusion.

Accommodation

India Gate is located in the heart of New Delhi, and there are plenty of accommodation options available nearby. Luxurious five-star hotels like The Leela Palace New Delhi, The Imperial, and Taj Mahal Hotel offer world-class amenities, while mid-range hotels such as The Park New Delhi, Shangri-La's - Eros Hotel, and The Claridges offer comfortable rooms and modern amenities. Budget hotels and hostels like Hotel Bright, Backpackers Hostel, and Zostel Delhi provide affordable options. Additionally, there are many Airbnb properties available, ranging from private rooms to entire apartments. It's advisable to book in advance, especially during peak tourist season, to secure the best deals and availability.
